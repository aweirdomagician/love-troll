There are people who enter your life like light
that filters through old cathedral glass —
changing the colour of everything it touches,
never knowing what it does to the dark.

✦   The Confession   ✦

I did not choose to fall for you —
the way one does not choose the dawn,
yet wakes to find the world
already changed by light.

✾ ✾ ✾

You move through rooms the way music moves —
you don't announce yourself,
you simply alter the atmosphere,
and suddenly everything
feels like it is held by something warmer.

✾ ✾ ✾

I have memorized the way you laugh
without meaning to.
I have learned the particular silence
that follows your name in my mind —
a silence that is not empty
but full, the way a held breath is full.

✾ ✾ ✾

This is my confession —
not a demand, not a question,
only this truth, set free at last:
I would cross every quiet ache
just to stand beside you
one ordinary evening.

✦   Pages I Never Sent   ✦

The first time

I don't remember exactly when it happened — only that one day you were simply there, and everything that came before felt slightly less vivid by comparison. That frightened me. It still does.

What I notice

The way you carry your quiet. The way you seem unaware of how the light keeps finding you, as though it too cannot help itself. I have begun to understand the poets. I have begun to understand why they wept.

What I have not said

That when I am near you I become more careful with my words — as though you are something fragile and rare that I am terrified to disturb. That your presence makes me want to be more than I am. That this, I think, is the kindest thing a person can do to another.

A truth I keep

There is a version of every quiet evening where you are there. I visit it often. I do not apologize for this. Some places we build in our hearts are too honest to feel like sins.

✦

You are the poem
I will spend years
not quite finishing.
